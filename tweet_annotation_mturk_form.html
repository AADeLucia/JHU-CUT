Identifying Event Tweets

Informed Consent Form:

Purpose of research study: We are collecting data to study how protest events are discussed on Twitter.
Benefits: This study may benefit society by improving our awareness of causes of protest events.
Risks: There are no risks for participating in this study.
Voluntary participation: You may stop participating at any time without penalty by clicking on the “Return HIT” button, or closing your browser window.
We may end your participation if you do not follow the instructions, or your answers significantly deviate from known answers.
Confidentiality: The only identifying information kept about you will be a WorkerID serial number and your IP address. This information may be disclosed to other researchers.
Questions/concerns: You may e-mail questions to the principle investigator, Dr. Mark Dredze.
If you feel you have been treated unfairly you may contact the Johns Hopkins University's Office of Human Research
Clicking on the “Accept HIT” button indicates that you understand the information in this consent form. You have not waived any legal rights you otherwise would have as a participant in a research study.

Instructions:

Read the tweet in the blue box at the top of each tab, then answer the 2-7 questions that follow.
Some questions will be greyed-out depending on your responses
After you have completed the final tweet click the Submit button to submit your responses.
You will not be able to submit until you have answered all required fields.
